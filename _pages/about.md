I have always been passionate about research, with a particular interest in marine biology. I earned a Bachelor's degree in Environmental Science from the University of Murcia (Spain), followed by a Master's degree in Fisheries Management and Aquaculture at the same university. After graduating, I joined the Marine Pollution Group at the Spanish Institute of Oceanography, where I gained research experience in marine ecotoxicology. I then moved to Girona (Spain) to pursue a PhD, which is currently my main research focus.

At the moment, my research focuses on assessing the status of the European sardine (*Sardina pilchardus*) in the Mediterranean Sea through the study of condition, reproductive biology, heavy metal contamination, biomarkers, and epigenetic mechanisms. I am conducting this research as part of my PhD under the supervision of [Dra Marta Munoz Frigola](https://www.udg.edu/ca/directori/pagina-personal?om=IG&IDP=153322&AP=1393&language=ca-ES), [Dr. Jordi Vinas](https://www.udg.edu/ca/directori/pagina-personal?ID=2000738&language=es-ES) and [Dr Odei Garcia Garin](https://www.udg.edu/ca/directori/pagina-personal?ID=89746&language=en-US).
